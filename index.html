Adjective v/s Adverb

ðŸ“š Adjectives and adverbs are two important parts of speech that help us add detail to our sentences.

Adjectives describe nouns (people, places, things) or pronouns by telling us more about them.
For example, in the sentence "The tall tree swayed in the wind," the word "tall" is an adjective that tells us more about the tree.

Adverbs, on the other hand, describe verbs (action words), adjectives, or even other adverbs. They often tell us how, when, where, or to what extent something happens.
For instance, in the sentence "The tree swayed gently in the wind" the word "gently" is an adverb that tells us how the tree swayed.
